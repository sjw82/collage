I propose a project bridging three arenas: data mining, collage, and web development. Each process builds upon the preceding; creating data, visualizing it, then making it interactive. The purpose of the project is to create an absurdist commentary on the postmodern nature of contemporary data management and processing with a visual focus on gender and sexuality. I’m pulling inspiration from the feminist dadaists Hannah Hoch and Barbara Kruger and their work dismantling both art and gender restrictions. This project begins with a database: GenderWatch. This database covers gender roles for both men and women, LGBTQ studies, family studies, and women’s studies with tens of thousands of documents. I would cluster articles into subtopics by key words and conduct all three steps of the project on each cluster separately.

A computer is able to identify patterns by reading massive amounts of text—a process known as data mining. Viewed as “distant reading” because of the scale, it is able to see patterns that a human simply couldn’t if they were to read as many documents, though it is not dissimilar to how humans read and compare texts. For my project I will be using the MALLET software which focuses on linguistic patterns with which I have some experience. For this project I will also undertake learning the computer language python in order to also conduct topic modelling which focuses on underlying semantic relationships. These two tools would reveal patterns in the database from both a statistical and literary point of view.

Having prepared the documents, learned Python, and conducted the analyses, I would move onto the visual aspect of the project: collage. Collage offers a neo dadaist approach, I argue the most germane approach, to the representation of mass data. Large collections of data are in my eyes absurdist, especially in that they hold immense power over the individual and yet are poorly understood by the layman. Neo dadaism is the 21st century resurgence of the dada “anti-art” movement that came about after World War I, now spurned by phenomena such as the foreboding of climate change and the ennui of the iphone generation. This project will retain several anti-art principles such as using recycled and found imagery; rejecting traditional ideals of beauty and rationality; and purporting anti-war, anti-bourgeois sentiment, leaning towards anarchism though rather than being focused on the military complex, I would focus on reclaiming the power of technology. This project seeks to make a claim about the meaning of technology and data management by observing it through a neo dadaist lense. I also anticipate finding similar themes within the database, particularly the rejection of traditional beauty and an anarchist slant. Using the results of the two forms of data mining, I will produce a collage, possibly mixed media depending on what patterns are identified, though primarily photo on canvas. There would be a number of separate collage panels that, when put together, create one unified collage though at least one panel will be solely digital.

The project goes one step further by adding an interactive element. The collage will be uploaded to a website, viewable in its entirety and every image, when clicked, would be accompanied by an explanation of why it was included. By doing this, the art escapes both time and place becoming dually postmodern in both technique and execution.
